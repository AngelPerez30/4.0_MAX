This component only asks for two data: production cost and units produced.
Production cost.


Production costs are monetary estimates of all expenses that have been made within the company, for the development of a good. These expenses include everything related to labor, costs of materials, as well as all indirect costs that in some way contribute to the manufacture of a good.


Units produced.


In this method, a fixed amount of depreciation is assigned to each unit of the production manufactured or used by the fixed asset. This method, in contrast to the straight line method, considers depreciation based on the use or activity, and not on time.
