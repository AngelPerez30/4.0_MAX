This component asks for 3 data, prime cost, direct manufacturing overhead, indirect costs.
Cousin cost

They are direct materials and direct labor. These costs are directly related to production. For example, the cost of labor (doctors, nurses, etc.) and direct materials (examination and treatment).


General manufacturing expenses.

they are related to the elaboration of the product, with its transformation, such as, for example, consumption of fuels, spare parts, amortization of fixed assets, supplies, repairs and conservation, etc. They will be attributed to the product totally or partially depending on the cost model we use, full cost, variable cost, rational allocation of structure charges, etc.


Indirect costs.


Indirect costs are those that are not directly attributable to the production of a particular product or service. Among them, some authors distinguish between the so-called semidirects, when in spite of not being able to be imputed to a specific product or service, they can be imputed to a specific cost center or department. And, the indirect ones, which would then be those that it is not possible to impute to a specific product or service, or to any specific cost center or department.
